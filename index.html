Deciphering Customer Sentiment in Food Delivery

An in-depth analysis of user ratings and review polarity across major food delivery platforms to uncover crucial areas for entrepreneurial focus.

Competitor Rating Dynamics

An initial look at the core competitors reveals stark differences in rating profiles, particularly the severity of negative feedback.

Key Findings

  • Deliveroo reviews show a J-shaped distribution, with most ratings clustering around 1 and 9.
  • Uber Eats displays a similar pattern, suggesting that negative experiences often drive customers to leave reviews.
  • Just Eat shows a bimodal distribution with high counts at Rating 1 and also at Ratings 4 and 5, indicating better issue resolution or satisfaction.
Actionable Insight Just Eat's profile is a benchmark for balancing negative feedback. New businesses should focus on excellent logistics and customer support.

Trustpilot Deep Dive

Rating, polarity, and correlation analysis from Trustpilot reviews.

Rating Distribution

The rating distribution is heavily skewed to Rating 1 indicating strong complaint bias.

Review Polarity Distribution

Text polarity centers around neutral to slightly negative sentiment, reflecting factual complaints over emotions.

Correlation Matrix

Moderate positive correlation (0.53) between rating and polarity; low subjectivity indicates objective, factual reviews.

Play Store Analysis

App experience vs. service experience comparison.

Rating Distribution

Polarised distribution: many 1-star and 5-star ratings indicate strong love-it-or-hate-it opinions.

Review Polarity Distribution

Positive text sentiment despite many 1-star ratings indicates mixed reviews focusing on different app or service aspects.

Correlation Matrix

Stronger correlation (0.68) between rating and sentiment, suggesting closer alignment of user rating and review text.

Review Volume Trends

Identifying key stress points over time.

Analysis

Spike in review volume late 2025 likely indicates either a service failure, system outage, or an aggressive push for review collection.

  • Interpretation A critical failure point requiring investigation and resolution.
  • Entrepreneurial Focus Scalability plans to handle spikes in reviews and complaints are essential.

Actionable Insights for FoodTech Entrepreneurs

Based on analysis, we recommend the following priorities for any new food delivery business:

1

Logistics Error Mitigation

Real-time tracking, AI-driven route optimization, and order verification systems to reduce failures and negative reviews.

2

Proactive Customer Support

Systems to detect and resolve issues before negative reviews are posted. Instant compensation improves customer satisfaction.

3

Differentiate App vs. Service Feedback

Separate feedback channels prevent confusion and maintain positive app reviews.

4

Focus on System Scalability

Infrastructure should support spikes in volume without crashing, ensuring long-term stability and growth.
